Rootine helps people stay consistent with small but important habits by combining a simple plant metaphor with social rewards.

Many people struggle with maintaining consistency in performing small (but important) tasks. There‚Äôs a lot of existing habit tracking apps out there (Finch was most common in user surveys), but we found in our needfinding that they have low rates of long-term retention. This was due to a number of factors, but often users would quit using a habit-tracking app (and therefore lose track of their tasks) due to it being overly complicated, a paywall, or completing the main ‚Äústory‚Äù associated with many apps. We found that users desired simplicity, positive reinforcement, and tangible rewards in a habit tracker. They also desired more customization with specifying the required timing of different habits.

Rootine aims to make habit tracking more motivating and sustainable. Each habit is represented by a virtual plant that grows when the user checks in. For every plant, the user or a friend can set a secret reward that only unlocks once the plant reaches harvest, turning consistency into something fun and rewarding.

Proposal & Needfinding

We started by understanding how people currently track habits and why they abandon habit apps.

We conducted surveys and interviews asking participants how they track habits, which apps they have tried, and what made them stop using those tools. This gave us a grounded view of real frustrations and desires, not just our own assumptions.

Key findings

  • Most participants had tried at least one habit app, but nearly all had stopped using it.
  • Popular ‚Äúvirtual pet‚Äù apps like Finch were motivating at first, but lost impact once the story or pet growth felt complete.
  • Participants were annoyed by cluttered interfaces, constant notifications, ads, and features locked behind paywalls.
  • People wanted something simple, customizable, and positive, with accountability to friends and meaningful rewards.

Design proposal

Based on these insights, we proposed Rootine: a mobile app where each habit is a customizable plant with a target ‚Äúharvest‚Äù date. The plant grows as the user completes their habit on schedule. For each plant, the user can either:

  • set a personal prize for themselves, or
  • send a link to a friend, who creates a secret gift using a simple companion website.

The friend‚Äôs reward can be a gift card code, an encouraging message, or a drawing. The user only sees this reward once they successfully keep the habit going through the harvest date.

Prototype

We iterated from early desktop sketches to a mobile-first flow and a web companion for friend rewards.

Our prototyping explored different ways to visualize habits, rewards, and customization. We started with desktop concepts, then shifted to a mobile app that keeps everything local to the device and feels more natural for day-to-day habit tracking.

Final Product

Our final product combines the Rootine mobile app with the web companion for friend rewards.

We ran a small usability study where participants used the prototype to create a habit, set a plant schedule, water it, harvest it, and send a reward to a friend. Overall, participants found the app easy to understand and liked the mix of visuals and rewards. Some suggested clearer feedback around when plants would be harvested and how streaks are counted, which we would explore in future iterations.
